The best books according to me

My favourite books are

Verity

"Verity" is a psychological thriller novel by Colleen Hoover that intertwines elements of suspense, romance, and the complexities of human nature. The story centers around Lowen Ashleigh, a struggling novelist who is given an unexpected opportunity to work on the remaining books in a successful series after the original author, Verity Crawford, becomes incapacitated following a tragic accident.

The five people you meet in heaven

"The Five People You Meet in Heaven", written by Mitch Albom, is a philosophical and heartwarming novel that explores themes of life, death, and the interconnectedness of human lives. The story follows Eddie, an elderly maintenance worker at an amusement park, who dies while trying to save a young girl from a falling ride.

The rain on the roof

"The Rain on the Rooftop" by Ruskin Bond is a reflective poem that captures the beauty and tranquility of rain. The speaker describes the soothing sounds of raindrops falling on the rooftop, which evoke a sense of nostalgia and fond memories.

Tuesdays with Morrie

"Tuesdays with Morrie", written by Mitch Albom, is a memoir that recounts the author’s experiences with his former college professor, Morrie Schwartz, who is diagnosed with ALS (amyotrophic lateral sclerosis). The book is structured around the visits that Mitch makes to Morrie every Tuesday, during which they discuss significant life lessons.

The kite runner

"The Kite Runner," written by Khaled Hosseini, is a poignant novel that explores themes of friendship, betrayal, redemption, and the complex nature of father-son relationships. Set against the backdrop of Afghanistan's turbulent history, the story follows Amir, a privileged boy from Kabul, and his best friend Hassan, the son of his father’s servant.
